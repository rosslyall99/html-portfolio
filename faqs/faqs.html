Getting Started

When Should I Get Started?

It is never too early to organising your kilt hire.

But recommended timings will depend on whether you are a solo hire or group booking.

Solo Hire

You can start to organise this whenever you like.

However, if you are looking for a specific outfit,
we recommend getting in touch about 3 months ahead of your function.

We cannot guarante availability until you have been measured.

Group Hire

You can start to organise this whenever you like.

If you are looking for a specific outfit,
we recommend getting in touch approximately 6 months ahead of your function.

However, if you would like to do this sooner, that is not an issue.

How Do I Get Started?

Solo Hire

Either make an appointment to come in and get measured,

Or complete our online self-measurement form.

We recommend in-store measurements if possible.

We cannot guarantee that we will be able to change garments
if incorrect sizes have been supplied.

Group Hire

Before getting in contact, please read our Communications section.

This section contains valuable advice on getting the very best service from Slanj
and how to avoid some of the problems that can arise with group bookings.

We recommend organising your party booking before bringing anybody in to get measured.

To get the process started, we recommend completing our Group Booking Enquiry Form.

Once we have arranged the party booking
we can organise fittings for your party members.

While it is possible for us to start the booking, process your function and outfit details,
and do all the measurements in one visit in-store, it is not advisable.

Fitting Appointments

Whether you are organising a group booking or a solo hire,
we advise you to make an appointment before coming in store to get measured.

You are welcome to come in without an appointment, but please be aware that
Those WITH an appointment will take priority over those WITHOUT.

While this is advisable during the week, it is essential at the weekend.
We are very busy at the weekend in both branches,
and regularly turn people away if we are fully booked.

To organise an appointment, please fill in our Appointment Request Form.

Collection & Return

Hire Duration

Our standard kilt hire duration is 4 nights.

We would normally suggest collecting the outfit 2 days before your function and returning 2 days after.

For example, if your function is on a Saturday, we would recommend a Thursday to Monday hire.

This gives you a couple of days to try on the outfit and come back to us, if any amendments are required.

It also allows you a couple of days after the function before returning the outfit to us.

If you need the outfit longer, the cost per outfit is £10 per night.

Collection

Solo Hire

Your kilt hire will be ready from 10:30 on your agreed collection date.

You do not need to make a collection appointment.

However, those with an appointment will take priority over those without.

Group Hire

We require all group bookings to make an appointment to collect their kilt hire.

If party members are picking up separately, please make multiple collection appointments

We will contact the party leader in the run up to the collection date to organise this.

We recommend as many of the party as possible attend this appointment.

Collecting from Duke Street is advisable as this is the where our main Hire Department is located.

In the event of any last minute amendments, it will be rectified quicker at Duke Street.

Identification

We require either a Drivers Licence or Passport as identification.

If party members are picking up separately, each solo/group will require identification.

If no identification is provided, we will not be able to release the outfit.

Return

Outfits can be returned to either branch.

Outfits can be returned early, but please be aware that Duke Street is closed on Sundays.

Outfits returned late will be subject to a daily £30 late fee.

Courier Delivery/Return Service

Unfortunately, we don’t currently offer a Hire Delivery service

However, we can assist getting the outfit ready for postage if you want to organise a courier to collect/return on your behalf.

Payment

Deposits

Solo Hire

To secure your booking, we will require a £30 deposit.

This is to be paid at the time of booking.

This is not a refundable security deposit; this is part payment for the kilt hire.

We accept cash or credit card payments in store.

Group Hire

To secure your booking, we will require a £30 deposit per outfit.

This is to be paid at the time of, or before, the fitting appointment.

This is not a refundable security deposit; this is part payment for the kilt hire.

Our preferred payment method for group bookings is online payment.

We recommend organising the party before the fitting appointment, so we can invoice you for the deposits.

We recommend that the party leader takes care of all payments as it simplifies the process.

But we appreciate that sometimes this isn’t possible, so we can accommodate multiple different people paying.

Final Payment

Solo Hire

Final Payment will be taken upon collection.

You can also settle the balance prior to collection. Contact us to arrange

Group Hire

We will contact the party leader about a week before collection to arrange final payment.

We prefer not to take final payment until then as prices can change if party members are added or removed.

Refunds

You can cancel your kilt hire(s) at any time.

If you cancel your hire(s) more than 14 days before the function you will be entitled to a full refund (except Hire Any Tartan).

If you cancel your hire(s) less than 14 days before the function you will be entitled to a full refund less £30 deposit (except Hire Any Tartan).

Hire Any Tartan hires must be cancelled within 7 days of booking otherwise no refund will be given.

GETTING IN TOUCH WITH SLANJ

Who Should Get In Touch?

Group bookings can be tricky.

They are organised months ahead of your function and there are generally many people involved.

We find that the best way to avoid mistakes is if we have one person in your group communicating with us.

If we have multiple people contacting us with different requirements, it can get quite confusing, and increase the chances of mistakes.

For a wedding, this will normally be the bride or groom, but if somebody else is nominated to be the party leader, that is not a problem.

How Should You Get In Touch?

Obviously, we have many ways of communicating with customers, but our preferred way of is via email.

Using email allows our sales advisors to focus solely on your enquiry.

It also means we both have a written record of what has been requested, should any confusions arise.

If you need to contact us urgently, please phone us, but we would ask you to speak to someone who is familiar with your booking.

For added peace of mind, please send us an email to confirm what has been discussed on the phone.

Please avoid using social media channels to discuss your booking, as the sales advisors dont have access to the channels.

Who Do You Speak To?

As soon as you have made your enquiry, we will assign a full-time member of staff to be your main point of contact.

They will contact you directly via email.

If at any stage you are not satisfied with the level of service you are receiving, we can offer you another sales advisor.

When Should I Get In Touch?

You can email any time you want – your dedicated sales advisors will respond as soon as they can.

If you need to phone us, please try an avoid the weekend.

Our shops are very busy at the weekend and while we do our best to respond to voicemails, it is not always possible.

At the risk of repeating myself, email is always the best option!
